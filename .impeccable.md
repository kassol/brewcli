## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are developers doing day-to-day local environment maintenance.
- Typical jobs: inspect installed packages, understand dependency relationships, update outdated packages, manage services/taps, and run cleanup tasks without memorizing awkward `brew` commands.
- Usage context is terminal-first, often during maintenance, debugging, upgrades, or environment repair. Speed, clarity, and trust matter more than visual novelty.

### Brand Personality
- Three-word personality: calm, professional, reliable.
- Emotional goal: help users feel in control of their machine state, not overwhelmed by Homebrew's rough edges.
- The interface should feel like a polished developer tool, not a flashy demo and not a faux desktop app squeezed into a terminal.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Direction: terminal-native, cold and composed, with refined information density.
- Keep the product clearly CLI/TUI-first while lifting the visual and interaction quality to a flagship standard.
- Avoid neon glow, cyberpunk gradients, and decorative effects that read as "trying too hard".
- Avoid over-GUI styling that makes the interface feel like a desktop window imitation.
- Accessibility posture: stable, high-contrast, minimal-motion, obvious focus and state changes.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize operational clarity over ornament.
2. Make hierarchy visible instantly through spacing, contrast, and structure.
3. Keep interactions calm and explicit; every action should feel trustworthy.
4. Preserve terminal-native character while removing rough, inconsistent edges.
5. Default to accessibility and legibility: strong contrast, clear focus, restrained motion.
